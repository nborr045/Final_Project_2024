How It Came to Be

In 1970 the band 'Black Sabbath' created the album 'Black Sabbath' by, for the first time, attempting to create music with distorted guitars and dark riffs. In that same year 'Black Sabbath' released their second album 'Paranoid' which was a bit faster in tempo and instrumentals. When listening to this album, a critic said that Black Sabbath's music sounds like a heavy metal falling, which is where the name of the genre came from. By the end of the 80's, there were about 11 metal genres that stemmed from Heavy Metal influences.

In Britain a movement began called 'New Wave of British Heavy Metal' where they used Punk music as inspiration and just made it faster. They usually used Ozzy Osbourne's (Black Sabbath's vocalist) vocal style but with faster instrumentals and music. Infamous Bands include Saxon, Iron Maiden, Judas Priest, etc.

Clothes Worn & Culture

Although this genre does not necessarily have a specific look, many people wore spandex during the 1980's when attending the concerts along with the band members themselves. This is until Judas Priest began to wear leather attires and it is now considered Heavy Metal's 'attire'.

Fun Facts

Although metal, along with all alternative subcultures, are supposed to be progressive, that was not necessarily the case for Heavy Metal. This genre attracted many racist, homophobic, and sexist cis, white men. When Judas Priest began their leather attire, so did the audience. In 1998 though Rob Halford (lead singer of Judas Priest) came out as gay and confessed that he took inspiration from outifts he saw at Gay clubs for his band's attires. Essentially, he made a bunch of homophobic white men wear outfits based on Gay attires. Iconic.
