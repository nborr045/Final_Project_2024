How It Came to Be

In 1970, the band 'Black Sabbath' created the album Black Sabbath by, for the first time, attempting to create music with distorted guitars and dark riffs. In that same year, 'Black Sabbath' released their second album, Paranoid, which featured faster tempos and instrumentals. When listening to this album, a critic said that Black Sabbath's music sounded like heavy metal falling, which is where the name of the genre originated. By the end of the 1980s, there were about 11 metal subgenres that stemmed from heavy metal influences.

In Britain, a movement called the 'New Wave of British Heavy Metal' began, using punk music as inspiration but making it faster. They often emulated Ozzy Osbourne's (Black Sabbath's vocalist) vocal style but paired it with faster instrumentals and music. Infamous bands include Saxon, Iron Maiden, and Judas Priest.

Clothes Worn & Culture

Although this genre does not necessarily have a specific look, many people wore spandex during the 1980s when attending concerts, along with the band members themselves. This continued until Judas Priest began wearing leather attire, which is now considered Heavy Metal's signature look.

Fun Facts

Although metal, along with all alternative subcultures, is supposed to be progressive, that was not necessarily the case for heavy metal. The genre attracted many racist, homophobic, and sexist cisgender white men. When Judas Priest began wearing leather attire, the audience followed. However, in 1998, Rob Halford (lead singer of Judas Priest) came out as gay and confessed that he took inspiration from outfits he saw at gay clubs for his band's attire. Essentially, he made a bunch of homophobic white men wear outfits based on gay fashion. Iconic.
