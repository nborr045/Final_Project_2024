How It Came to Be

The thrash metal band Anthrax originated in New York. In the 1990s, with hip-hop being hugely popular in the city, they decided to create a song called I'm the Man, in which they intentionally rapped poorly. This song became a staple of their live shows for a while. Beginning in the early 2000s, a band called Limp Bizkit took inspiration from this concept and incorporated rap with metal instrumentals, being one of the first metal bands to use a 7-string guitar. The name "nu-metal" comes from "new metal," reflecting the new sound of the 2000s. This movement was followed by bands like Linkin Park, Korn, and Slipknot.

Clothes Worn & Culture

From this genre emerged the "Mall Goth" aesthetic. This style consisted of extremely baggy clothes and caps, often incorporating elements of a skater or gothic look. The term "Mall Goth" originated because teenagers would frequently hang out at malls dressed in this fashion. At the time, traditional metal fans often regarded this aesthetic as a "poser" style.

Fun Facts

A wrestler named The Undertaker used Limp Bizkit's song "Rollin'" as his entrance song for some time during his wrestling matches.
