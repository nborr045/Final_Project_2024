How It Came to Be

Thrash Metal band 'Anthrax' was from New York. In the 90s, Hip-Hop was very popular in New York, so they decided to make a song called 'I'm The Man' in which they purposefully rap badly. This became a staple of their shows for a while. Beggining in the early 2000s a band called 'Limp Bizkit' took inspiration from this and incorporated Rap with metal instrumentals (first time in metal doing 7 string in a guitar). The name Nu-Metal comes from 'New Metal' as it was the new sound of the 2000s. After this followed bands such as Linkin Park, Korn, and Slipknot.

Clothes Worn & Culture

Although this genre does not have a specific look, it is very theatrical in nature. The sounds are dramatic and the performances tend to have a lot of pyrotechnics and props. For example, the band 'Rammstein' often takes inspiration from plays they saw growing up in their concert theatrics.

Fun Facts

From this genre started the 'Mall Goth' aesthetic. This aesthetuc consisted of really baggy clothes and caps. Sometimes this included a skateboy/girl look or a gothic look. This started due to teenagers walking around in mall being dressed like this, hence 'Mall Goth'. Back then, metal fans cosidered this aesthetic a 'poser' aesthetic.
