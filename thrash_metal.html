How It Came to Be

As a response to the rise of Heavy Metal's subgenre of Glam Metal, Thrash Metal begun. Glam metal was commercialized, Mainstream and Radio-Friendly while Thrash Metal was raw, underground, and completely rebellious. The name comes from the way people would thrash around at Mosh Pits. The 'Big 4' are considered Metallica, Megadeth, Slayer and Anthrax as they are the first four bands in thrash metal to get a record label. They are also considered a staple for any metal fan.

Clothes Worn & Culture

In this genre people would usually wear jeans and long hair. The staple of the genre were jean vests filled with patches. People would sow or iron the patches of their favorite bands all around the vest. The vests usually had a big patch on the back that would signify the wearer's favorite band.

Fun Facts

Two of the 'Big 4' bands actually began due to a rivalry. Megadeth's lead singer, Dave Mustaine, was once part of Metallica's original members as the lead guitarist. He wrote most riffs and is responsible for some of the bands most famous tracks such as in the album 'Master of Puppets'. Despite this, he had a bad persoality when drunk, and Metallica at the time drank very often. Because of this, Dave Mustaine was kicked out of Metallica and replaced with an ex-member of the band 'Exodus'. As a form of revenge, Dave Mustaine formed his own band, Megadeth, and sometimes used the same riffs he used in Metallica in a different song due to being the original creator. Although this rivalry between them and fans lasted for years, the bands are now reconciled and recognize they were both important for the history of metal.
