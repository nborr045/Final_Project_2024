How It Came to Be

The band Death set out to use the sound of thrash metal but aimed to make it faster, heavier, and with more guttural vocals. Eventually, the lyrics and music became so heavy that the vocal style of thrash metal no longer fit. The band Possessed then released their album Seven Churches, in which they incorporated guttural vocals, solidifying the sound of the genre. In this same album, they included a song called "Death Metal," and, combined with the influences of the band Death, it helped solidify the name of the genre. Most lyrics in this genre are centered around gore. Other influential bands include Obituary, Morbid Angel, and Cannibal Corpse.

Clothes Worn & Culture

This genre did not have a specific look. Males typically had long hair but did not wear makeup or flashy clothes. They would usually wear a band t-shirt and jeans. Despite this, thanks to Cannibal Corpse's second lead singer, George "Corpsegrinder" Fisher, the "windmill" move became popular in the scene. The windmill move consists of one (usually with long hair) moving their head in circles so their hair spins like a windmill.

Fun Facts

The band Cannibal Corpse appeared in the movie Ace Ventura: Pet Detective. In the film, Jim Carrey's character is out of place at a death metal concert. Scenes show people crowd surfing and moshing, and Carrey's character comically dances to the music, highlighting the contrast between his character and the intense atmosphere of the concert.
