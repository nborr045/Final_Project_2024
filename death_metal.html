How It Came to Be

The band 'Death' set out to use the sound of Thrash Metal but wanted to make it faster, heavier, and with more guttural vocals. Eventually, the lyrics and music was so heavy that the vocal style of Thrash Metal did not fit anymore. The band 'Possessed' then released their album 'Seven Churches' in which they had guttural vocals, solidifying the sound of the genre. In this same album, they had a song called 'Death Metal', mixed with the influences of the band 'Death' it solidified the name of the genre. Most lyrics in this genre have to do with gore. Other influential bands include 'Obituary', 'Morbid Angel', and 'Cannibal Corpse'.

Clothes Worn & Culture

This genre did not have a certain look. Males would have long hair but would not wear any makeup or flashy clothes. They would usually wear a band t-shirt and some jeans. Despite this, thanks to Cannibal Corpse's second lead singer 'George Corpsegrinder Fisher', the 'windmill' became popular in the scene. The windmill move consists of one (usually with long hair) moving their head in circles so their hair moves like a windmill.
